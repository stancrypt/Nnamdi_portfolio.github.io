Global Health and Immunization Intelligence Dashboard

This project combines SQL analytics, interactive data visualization, and machine learning to explore how immunization coverage, mortality rates, disease burden, and socioeconomic conditions influence life expectancy across countries and regions. Using global public health datasets, the project analyzes key indicators such as Polio, Hepatitis B, and Diphtheria vaccination coverage, infant and adult mortality, HIV incidence, GDP per capita, schooling, and life expectancy to identify health disparities, vulnerable populations, and emerging public health risks.

1. SQL Data Analysis & Transformation

Using SQL Server Management Studio, the raw datasets were cleaned, transformed, and analyzed to generate meaningful public health insights. SQL queries were used to perform data validation, create health risk classifications, calculate vaccination and mortality trends, and prepare optimized views for reporting and visualization. The analysis focused on identifying relationships between vaccination coverage, mortality rates, economic development, and population health outcomes.

2. Interactive Power BI Dashboard

An interactive dashboard was developed in Power BI to visualize global health patterns and support data-driven decision-making. The dashboard includes regional health comparisons, vaccination coverage trends, mortality analysis, socioeconomic indicators, and risk intelligence monitoring. Users can explore high-risk countries, compare developed and developing economies, and analyze how healthcare access and immunization performance impact life expectancy over time.

3. Predictive Modeling with Scikit-learn

To extend the analysis, a machine learning regression model was developed using Scikit-learn to predict life expectancy based on health, vaccination, and socioeconomic indicators. Models including Linear Regression and Random Forest Regressor were trained and evaluated using metrics such as R² Score, MAE, and RMSE. Feature importance analysis revealed the strongest predictors of life expectancy, providing deeper insight into the factors that most significantly influence global health outcomes.

Correlation Analysis Result

The following correlation analysis was run to examine the connection between various variable in the dataset

Correlation Analysis: Life Expectancy, GDP per Capita, and Schooling

A correlation analysis was conducted to examine the relationship between life expectancy, economic conditions, and education levels across countries. The results revealed a strong positive correlation between schooling and life expectancy (r = 0.73), indicating that countries with higher levels of education generally experience longer average life expectancy. This suggests that access to education may contribute to improved health awareness, healthcare utilization, and overall living conditions. The analysis also showed a moderate positive correlation between GDP per capita and life expectancy (r = 0.58), implying that countries with stronger economic performance tend to have better health outcomes and longer survival rates. Similarly, GDP per capita and schooling demonstrated a moderate positive relationship (r = 0.58), suggesting that economically developed countries often have higher educational attainment levels.
Overall, the findings highlight the important role of socioeconomic development and education in influencing population health outcomes and improving life expectancy globally.

Correlation Analysis: Polio Vaccination Coverage and Mortality Indicators

A correlation analysis was performed to examine the relationship between polio vaccination coverage and key mortality indicators, including infant deaths, under-five deaths, and adult mortality. The results showed strong negative correlations between polio vaccination coverage and both infant deaths (r = -0.74) and under-five deaths (r = -0.74). This indicates that countries with higher polio immunization coverage generally experience lower child mortality rates, highlighting the critical role of vaccination programs in improving child survival outcomes. Polio vaccination coverage also demonstrated a moderate negative correlation with adult mortality (r = -0.52), suggesting that stronger healthcare systems and immunization performance may contribute to broader improvements in population health. Additionally, infant deaths and under-five deaths showed an extremely strong positive correlation (r = 0.99), reflecting the close relationship between these two child mortality indicators. Both indicators also showed strong positive correlations with adult mortality, indicating that countries with poor child health outcomes often experience higher overall mortality levels.
Overall, the analysis emphasizes the significant association between immunization coverage and reduced mortality, reinforcing the importance of vaccination programs and healthcare access in improving public health outcomes.

Correlation Analysis: HIV Incidence, Alcohol Consumption, and Adult Mortality

A correlation analysis was conducted to evaluate the relationship between HIV incidence, alcohol consumption, and adult mortality across countries. The results revealed a strong positive correlation between HIV incidence and adult mortality (r = 0.70), indicating that countries with higher rates of HIV infections tend to experience significantly higher levels of adult mortality. This finding reflects the substantial impact of infectious diseases on population health and survival outcomes, particularly in regions with limited healthcare access and treatment coverage. Alcohol consumption showed a very weak negative correlation with HIV incidence (r = -0.03), suggesting little to no direct relationship between the two variables within the dataset. Additionally, alcohol consumption demonstrated a weak negative correlation with adult mortality (r = -0.24), indicating that alcohol consumption alone may not be a strong predictor of mortality outcomes when compared with major health burdens such as HIV.
Overall, the analysis highlights HIV incidence as a major factor associated with increased adult mortality, reinforcing the importance of disease prevention, treatment access, and public health interventions in improving adult survival rates globally.
